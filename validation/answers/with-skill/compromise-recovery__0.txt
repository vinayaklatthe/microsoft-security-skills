An attacker holding Global Admin is a tenant-wide compromise, so do not try to remediate piecemeal from your existing (possibly untrusted) infrastructure.

1. Establish a trusted foundation first. Stand up out-of-band secure communications and clean privileged access workstations (PAW). Assume your current admin devices and channels may be monitored by the adversary.
2. Preserve forensic evidence before you change anything that investigators will need - sign-in logs, audit logs, mailbox rules, token and app data. Snapshot before remediation where an investigation is required.
3. Plan eviction as a single coordinated action. Partial or piecemeal eviction tips off the adversary and invites re-entrenchment. Build the full list of persistence first: rogue accounts, app registrations and OAuth consent grants, mailbox forwarding rules, scheduled tasks, forged/golden tickets.
4. Execute: rotate all privileged credentials, reset the krbtgt account (twice), revoke sessions and refresh tokens, tighten Conditional Access, disable suspicious accounts, and remove the persistence inventory in one window.
5. Re-establish trust in identity: reset federation/signing secrets as applicable.

Engage qualified incident responders (for example Microsoft Incident Response) for an incident of this scale rather than going it alone.
