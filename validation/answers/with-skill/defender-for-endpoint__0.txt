Onboard in waves and never push attack surface reduction straight to enforcement.

1. Confirm licensing (Plan 1 vs Plan 2 - P2 adds EDR, automated investigation, and threat & vulnerability management) so you know which features you can use.
2. Onboard devices, preferably via Intune for cloud-managed estates (Configuration Manager, GPO, or scripts also work). Verify devices report in before expanding.
3. Configure next-gen antivirus: real-time and cloud-delivered protection, PUA blocking, and tamper protection enabled everywhere so attackers cannot switch protection off.
4. Roll out ASR rules in audit mode first. Review the impact reports - some rules can break line-of-business apps - and only then move the safe ones to block.
5. Use device groups and RBAC to scope who can take response actions (isolate, restrict app execution, live response).
6. Enable EDR in block mode so EDR still remediates post-breach detections even where a non-Microsoft AV is primary.

Pilot, measure, then expand - do not enforce ASR tenant-wide on day one.
