Respond fast on the device, then reduce the exposure that allowed it.

Immediate response:
1. Isolate the device from the network through Defender for Endpoint so the attacker loses interactive access while you investigate. Use full isolation for active threats.
2. Run an antivirus scan and, if needed, restrict app execution (only Microsoft-signed binaries) to stop further payloads.
3. Collect the investigation package for forensics, and review the device timeline in the incident to understand entry point and lateral movement.
4. Let automated investigation and remediation (AIR) act on what it can, and confirm remediation.

Reduce future exposure:
5. Use threat & vulnerability management to see which vulnerabilities and misconfigurations on that device (and similar devices) gave the attacker an opening, and prioritise remediation by exposure and active threat.
6. Confirm tamper protection and EDR in block mode are on, and tighten the ASR rules relevant to the technique used.

Scope all response actions with device groups and RBAC so the right team acts on the right machines.
