For privileged role activation, set controls that prove the user is legitimate, the task is justified, and the elevation is temporary.

1. Require MFA on activation
- Require MFA every time a privileged role is activated.
- For the highest roles, prefer phishing-resistant methods such as FIDO2 security keys or certificate-based authentication.

2. Require justification
- Make users enter a business reason for activation.
- Keep the justification in audit history for review.

3. Require approval for sensitive roles
- Use approval for Global Administrator, Privileged Role Administrator, Security Administrator, Application Administrator, Cloud Application Administrator, Conditional Access Administrator, Exchange Administrator, and roles that can change authentication or access policy.
- Use trained approvers, not broad distribution lists.

4. Keep activation duration short
- Use the shortest practical duration.
- One to two hours is a good default for highly privileged roles.
- Avoid all-day activations unless the role genuinely needs it.

5. Require ticket information where possible
- If you use ITSM, require a ticket number for activation.
- Use this to connect privileged access to an approved change or incident.

6. Send notifications
- Notify the user, approvers, and security operators when sensitive roles are activated.
- Alert on activation outside business hours or from unusual locations.

7. Scope access tightly
- Use administrative units or narrower roles when possible.
- Do not use Global Administrator for tasks that have a specific admin role.

8. Review access regularly
- Use access reviews for eligible assignments.
- Remove inactive, stale, or unnecessary eligible admins.
- Review PIM audit history for repeated or unusual activation.

The strongest configuration is phishing-resistant MFA, approval, justification, ticket number, short duration, notification, and recurring access review.
