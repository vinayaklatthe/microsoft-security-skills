Build the Purview DLP policy in stages. Start by detecting sensitive data, then notify users, then restrict sharing once false positives are understood.

1. Define the scope
- Identify the sensitive information types, such as credit card numbers, national IDs, health records, financial data, or confidential project names.
- Decide which locations are in scope: Exchange email, SharePoint, OneDrive, Teams, and endpoints.
- Endpoint DLP requires onboarded devices and the right licensing, commonly Microsoft 365 E5 or equivalent compliance licensing.

2. Create the policy
- In Microsoft Purview compliance portal, go to Data loss prevention.
- Create a custom policy or start from a template.
- Select the locations you want to protect.
- Limit the first rollout to pilot users or groups.

3. Define rules
- Add conditions for sensitive information types, sensitivity labels, trainable classifiers, or keywords.
- Use confidence levels and instance counts to reduce noise.
- Add exceptions for known business processes, approved domains, or trusted groups.

4. Configure actions
- Start in test mode with policy tips.
- Notify users when they are about to share sensitive data.
- Allow business justification or override during pilot if appropriate.
- Then block or restrict external sharing, email sending, copying to USB, printing, or upload to unsupported cloud services as needed.

5. Endpoint controls
- For endpoints, configure restrictions for copy to removable media, print, clipboard, network share, remote desktop, and browser uploads.
- Use endpoint groups so you can pilot with a small set of devices.

6. Alerts and reporting
- Configure alerts for high-severity events.
- Review Activity explorer and DLP alerts.
- Track false positives, overrides, business justification, and repeat offenders.

7. Roll out safely
- Pilot in audit or test mode.
- Tune rules and exceptions.
- Move from notify to block for high-confidence scenarios.
- Expand users, locations, and endpoint controls gradually.

The goal is not to block all sharing on day one. It is to protect high-risk data movement while giving users clear guidance and keeping business processes working.
